Safety- and Fidelity-Regularized Diffusion for HD-Map-Conditioned Traffic Scene Initialization

Project overview and qualitative results for a diffusion model that initializes multi-agent traffic scenes from vectorized HD maps using geometry-aware attention and training-time validity regularization.

Authors: Dae Jung Kim, Seulbin Hwang, Jinhan Lee Affiliations: NAVER LABS. Status: Under Review
Manuscript Status Main Table GIF Gallery DOI / PDF (post-acceptance)

SFiD-Init combines lane-graph map reasoning with geometry-aware diffusion denoising to sample plausible multi-agent starting scenes from HD maps. Training-time validity shaping reduces immediate collisions and off-road placements, while fidelity terms help preserve lane-consistent scene structure.

At a glance

Goal. Formulate HD-map-conditioned traffic scene initialization as conditional diffusion generation of joint multi-agent initial states satisfying lane topology/semantics and physical validity.

Key idea. Topology-aware Map Encoder + interaction-aware Agent Encoder + diffusion Transformer denoiser with continuous geometric relation biases (IAGE/AMGE), regularized by a differentiable safety-shaped loss and fidelity-preserving objectives.

Evaluation. Distribution similarity (JSD-based) and safety rates (collision/off-road/ttc<1s) on nuPlan, WOMD, and unseen Hwaseong LivingLab map.

Project Summary

SFiD-Init focuses on the first step of simulation: sampling a realistic multi-agent starting layout from an HD map instead of hand-authoring one. The model combines lane-topology encoding, interaction-aware agent encoding, and geometry-aware diffusion denoising so that sampled vehicles stay aligned with road structure while reducing obviously unusable initial states. Across nuPlan, WOMD, and an unseen real-world map, the resulting scenes remain diverse, lane-consistent, and better suited for downstream rollout and evaluation.

Method

Summary

  • Input: Vectorized HD map (lane graph with predecessor/successor/adjacent relations) and desired number/types of agents.
  • Map Encoder: Topology-aware sparse message-passing attention over lane graphs with optional FiLM-based dynamic context (e.g., traffic signal phases).
  • Agent Encoder: IAGE-biased self-attention captures interaction-aware agent embeddings as timestep-unconditioned priors.
  • Diffusion Denoiser: AdaLN-conditioned Transformer iteratively refines noisy agent states via IAGE self-attention, AMGE agent–map cross-attention, and a pointwise FFN.
  • Training: Diffusion objective + differentiable safety-shaped loss (collision/off-road) + fidelity regularizers (contrastive map-agent alignment, masked map reconstruction) to reduce invalidity while mitigating distribution drift.
  • Output: Diverse, physically valid and map-consistent joint multi-agent initial scene configurations for downstream simulation.

Key components

IAGE
Inter-Agent Geometric relation bias Encoder — additive attention bias in agent self-attention with continuous pairwise geometric relations.
AMGE
Agent–Map Geometric relation bias Encoder — additive attention bias in cross-attention between agents and map latents.
Safety
Differentiable safety-shaped loss penalizing collision and off-road violations during training.
Fidelity
Contrastive map–agent alignment + masked map reconstruction to prevent distribution drift under safety regularization.
